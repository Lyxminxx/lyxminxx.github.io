The smartphone market today feels like it’s forgotten how to dream. Once full of charm and character, it’s now a parade of nearly identical metal slabs, each one slightly shinier and more expensive than the last. One has a half-eaten apple on it, so I guess that’s the one I’m supposed to want? What happened to meaningful design—tech that tells a story, that _stands out_?

Out of all this noise, one company keeps catching my eye: Nothing. With bold designs, transparent hardware, and actual ideas about how phones should _feel_ to use, they’re doing something refreshingly different. With the upcoming release of the Nothing Phone (3) and Headphone (1), I wanted to dig into what makes them special—and why the rest of the industry should be paying attention.

## Phones are the product, not me!
A big problem with phones today is that they aren't a tool, you are. When a notification on your phone comes in, you *have to* check it. Bright lights and a notification icon come up and it's distracting. Then you open that one message to tell your mom what you want for dinner, and before you know it you're in doomscroll hell. Is this what we want out of the best tool made? When did we stop being the user and start being the used? 

Nothing does something interesting with this problem, they saw the issues of the phone using us and decided to be the solution. One of the distinguishing features of the Nothing phones so far has been the glyph interface. It's a set of lights on the back that for example lights up when you get a notification. You can also set specific lights for different people, this means you can know if you need to check that notification without ever looking at your screen. That one single feature is a great solution already! But, with the launch of the Phone (3) we see a different approach to the problem. A small screen on the back that shows pixel art. This will make it even easier to see who is messaging you.
